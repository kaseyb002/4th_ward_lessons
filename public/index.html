Saving Ordinances Will Bring Us Marvelous Light

Spanish Fork 4th Ward - April 15, 2018

Talk given by Elder Wakolo - April 2018 General Conference

From talk:

Participating in ordinances and honoring the associated covenants will bring you marvelous light and protection in this ever-darkening world.

Section 1

Q: What are some of the blessings that come from keeping your ordinary, everyday commitments?

Q: How are temple covenants different than commitments we make with other people, or with ourselves?

Q: What are some of the consequences of repeatedly breaking our covenants?

Q: Why would the Lord command us to make covenants when we are not capable of perfectly keeping those covenants?

Section 2

From talk:

After attending The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints for 39 years, John was baptized in 2015. A year later, John and Bonnie were sealed in the Memphis Tennessee Temple, 20 years after she had received her own endowment. Their 47-year-old son, Robert, said of his dad, “Dad has really, really blossomed since he received the priesthood.” Bonnie added, “John has always been a happy and cheerful person, but receiving the ordinances and honoring his covenants has enhanced his gentleness.”

Q: How do your covenants affect your everyday life?

Section 3

From talk:

in 1842, the Prophet Joseph Smith administered the endowment to a limited number of men and women. Mercy Fielding Thompson was one of them. The Prophet said to her, “This [endowment] will bring you out of darkness into marvelous light.

Q: How do the ordinances bring light into our lives?

D&C 84:20

20 Therefore, in the ordinances thereof, the power of godliness is manifest.

Without being too specific about what happens in the temple, how do we learn to become like God in the temple ordinances?

From talk:

We must also, in President Russell M. Nelson’s words, “keep on the covenant path.” President Nelson continued, “Your commitment to follow the Savior by making covenants with Him and then keeping those covenants will open the door to every spiritual blessing and privilege available to men, women, and children everywhere.”

Q: What does it mean to keep your covenants? Is it just holding a temple recommend?
